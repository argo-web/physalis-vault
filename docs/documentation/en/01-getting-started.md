# Getting Started

Welcome to **Physalis**, your organisation's secrets vault.
This page walks you through everything from receiving your invitation to
your first secure login.

## Understanding Physalis URLs

Physalis is **multi-tenant**: each client organisation has its own
isolated subdomain.

| URL                                | Purpose                                        |
|------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------|
| `<your-slug>.physalis.cloud`       | Your login, your dashboard — this is where you live |
| `vault.physalis.cloud`             | The platform's super-admin portal — you'll normally never go there |

The `<slug>` is the short name for your organisation (e.g. `argoweb`,
`scroll`…). It is defined by the super-admin when the client account is
created and appears in the invitation link you receive.

> 💡 **Bookmark** : add your subdomain to your favourites on your first login.
> It's your single entry point.

## 1. Receive and accept the invitation

An admin member of your organisation has invited you by email
(via Mailgun, from `noreply@physalis.cloud`).

The email contains an activation link **valid for 48 hours**. If it expires,
ask the admin to send a new invitation — the previous link becomes unusable.

Clicking the link takes you to an account creation page:

- **Email**: pre-filled, not editable (tied to the invitation)
- **Full name**: free-form
- **Password**: minimum 12 characters recommended, mix of letters / numbers / symbols

On submission, your account is created, you are added to the organisation
with the role predefined by the admin (see [Organisations & roles](organisations-and-roles)),
and you are automatically logged in.

## 2. Log in from your subdomain

Every time you return to Physalis, go directly to **`<your-slug>.physalis.cloud/login`**
(never to `vault.physalis.cloud` — that portal does not accept
organisation user accounts).

If you try to log in on the wrong domain, you will get an invalid
credentials error even if your password is correct.

## 3. Enable 2FA (strongly recommended)

2FA (two-factor authentication) adds a 6-digit code generated by your
phone at each login. Without it, your password alone protects all of
your secrets.

**To enable it:**

1. Click on your email in the top-right corner of the dashboard → you land on
   `/settings/security`.
2. Under **"Two-factor authentication"**, click **"Enable 2FA"**.
3. Scan the QR code with a TOTP app:
   - **Bitwarden / Vaultwarden** (built into the password manager)
   - **1Password**, **Authy**, **Google Authenticator**, **Aegis** (Android)
   - Or directly in your Physalis vault (see [Vaults](vaults))
4. Enter the 6-digit code shown by the app to confirm.
5. **Save the 8 backup codes** shown only once. Store them in your Physalis
   vault or another safe place — they are the only way to recover access
   if you lose your phone.

On your next login, after entering your email and password, the TOTP code
will be requested on the same screen (single-step UX).

> ⚠️ **The browser extension requires 2FA** to authenticate. If you plan
> to install the extension, enable 2FA first.

## 4. Explore the dashboard

Once logged in, the top navigation bar gives you access to:

- **Projects** — your organisation's applications, their environments,
  and their secrets ([→ Projects & environments](projects-and-environments))
- **🔒 Personal vault** — your private vault for credentials not tied
  to a project (see [Vaults](vaults))
- **📤 Shares** — share a secret via a single-use link, or ask a third
  party to send you a secret in encrypted form
  ([→ Shares](shares))
- **📖 Documentation** — the docs you are reading right now

The **active organisation** (if you belong to several) can be switched via
the selector in the top-left corner.

## 5. What next?

- **You are a developer** → install the [browser extension](browser-extension)
  for auto-fill of credentials on your websites.
- **You are an admin of your organisation** → read
  [Organisations & roles](organisations-and-roles) to invite other
  members and configure permissions.
- **You are setting up a CI/CD deployment** → read
  [OIDC Deployment](oidc-deployment).

## Forgotten password

On the login page, the **"Forgot password?"** link sends an email
with a reset link valid for 1 hour. You can choose a new password without
knowing the old one — make sure you have access to your inbox.

> If you have 2FA enabled and have also lost your phone,
> use one of your **backup codes** to pass 2FA right after
> resetting your password.
